Course content:

The course will cover the fundamental concepts, building blocks, and design principles of the blockchain technology. A substantial part of the course focuses on the challenges in constructing a blockchain, especially from the security and privacy perspectives. It is designed to equip students with theoretical as well as practical knowledge of blockchain and web3 development. The course is especially useful for students who plan to do research and/or product development in the area of distributed ledger technology. Some key topics covered in the course are:

  • Consensus in DLT
  • Addresses and wallets
  • Bitcoin scripts
  • Ethereum smart contracts
  • EVM execution model
  • Solidity, ABI specification
  • Token standards (ERC-20, ERC-721, ERC-1155)
  • DApps, oracles, decentralized storage
  • Secure smart contracts development
  • Blockchain scaling solutions
PS: Course content may vary depending on the interest and response from the students.

Group project:

Each group (group size to be decided) will work on a programming project. Implement a DApp with Solidity and Web3. Submit a single zip file that contains: (1) source code; (2) screenshots of code execution; and (3) clear instructions (in README.txt) to set up environment (if any) and execute the code. No report is required.

Policies:

Do and submit your original work only. Cheating, plagiarism, and academic dishonesty will carry consequences.
